High Speed Photography References

If you would like to learn more about the technology behind high speed photography, please check out this page I have put together. As with the rest of this website, it's still very WIP, but I hope that it will be a comprehensive resource that gives a thorough understanding of the various types of high speed camears while still being light on math. There is a (perhaps surprising) lack of easy to follow information about high speed cameras so I hope that this resource will be informative and interesting.

Here's a load of papers and resources that I've gathered related to the fascinating field of high speed photography.
Most of it pertains to multiple frame imaging of an event, not the single flash single frame photography some (rather erroneously) call "high speed photography".

Since there are many different types of high speed cameras, I've categorized them as follows:

  1. Rotating mirror optical compensation cameras
  2. Image dissection cameras (all types)
  3. Rotating prism/rotating drum framing cameras
  4. Rotating mirror streak cameras
  5. Image tube based systems (streak and framing)
  6. Digital high speed photography
  7. Other

Some papers fall under numerous categories so please bear with my rather arbitrary categorizing.
The perceptive viewer may notice that these are very similar to the chapter divisions Dubovik uses in The Photographic Recording of High-Speed Processes. I would personally consider his work to be the most comprehensive guide to high-speed photography ever published.

I've included (when applicable) the name of the paper, authors, date, category, and a Sci-Hub link if available.
