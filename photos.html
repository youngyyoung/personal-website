I'm very passionate about photography and imaging of all kinds, but most of my casual photography is focused on "aesthetic" pictures.

Equipment

My most productive camera by far is my Nikon F5 - I bought it for $50 a couple years ago on Craigslist and it's a joy to use. I'm currently looking to upgrade to a digital camera but that'll have to wait a while.

I have a darkroom and used to process (C-41) and print (RA-4) my film myself, but unfortunately I don't have the time for it nowadays so I have to pay outrageous prices for processing at a lab.

At any rate, 35mm is a bit of a resolution dead-end, especially with color. People love to claim that normal 35mm film can get 24 megapixels or higher, but that's simply not true in most cases. A lot of lenses can barely resolve 24 MP, let alone the film.
